Introduction to Digital Literacy

Module Overview

Digital literacy is the ability to use digital technologies such as smartphones, computers, and the internet to access information, communicate, solve problems, and participate effectively in society. This module introduces learners to basic digital concepts and builds confidence in using digital tools safely and effectively.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, participants will be able to:

  • Define digital literacy and explain its importance.
  • Identify common digital devices and their uses.
  • Demonstrate basic smartphone and computer operations.
  • Navigate the internet to find information.
  • Use digital communication tools responsibly.

Understanding Digital Devices

Learning Outcomes

Participants will be able to:

  • Identify different digital devices.
  • Describe the functions of common devices.
  • Understand how digital devices support daily life.

What is a Digital Device?

A digital device is an electronic tool that helps people create, store, process, and share information.

Common Digital Devices

Device Purpose
Smartphone Communication, internet access, mobile banking, learning
Computer/Laptop Work, education, document creation, internet access
Tablet Learning, entertainment, communication
Feature Phone Calls, SMS, mobile money
Smart TV Accessing digital content and information

How Digital Devices Help Communities

Digital devices can be used for:

  • Accessing educational materials
  • Communicating with family and friends
  • Conducting business
  • Accessing health information
  • Receiving agricultural updates
  • Finding employment opportunities
  • Accessing government services

Practical Activity: Device Identification Exercise

  • Identify digital devices that are available to you.
  • Name their parts.
  • How they are used in everyday life?
  • Which digital devices do you use regularly?
  • How do these devices help your family or business?
  • What challenges do you face when using digital devices?

Internet Navigation

Learning Outcomes

Participants will be able to:

  • Explain what the internet is.
  • Connect to the internet.
  • Search for information online.
  • Identify trustworthy information.
What is the Internet?

The internet is a global network that connects people and information around the world. The internet allows users to:

  • Access information on the world wide web
  • Communicate with others online
  • Learn new skills
  • Conduct business
  • Access online services
Ways to Connect to the Internet
  • Mobile data from your phone plan
  • Wi-Fi networks (from home or businesses)
  • Community internet centers
Understanding Web Browsers

A web browser helps users access websites. Some of the most commonly used web browsers include:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Microsoft Edge
Using Search Engines

A search engine helps users find information online. Some of the most commonly used search engines include:

  • Google
  • Bing
  • Yahoo!
Effective Searching

To search the internet, access a web browser. Then, using a search engine, type your search information in the Search bar. The search bar is normaly indicated by a magnifying glass. Example searches include:

  • "Maize farming tips Zambia"
  • "Health clinics near me"
  • "Online learning courses"
Identifying Reliable Information

Always check your sources! Key things to look at when determining if the information that you find is reliable:

  • Source of information
  • Publication date
  • Author credibility
  • Multiple sources confirming the same information
Avoiding Misinformation

Do not immediately trust:

  • Sensational headlines. These are often referred to as "click bait", intentionally worded to make you want to click on it. These normally turn out to be misleading.
  • Unverified social media posts
  • Messages asking for money urgently
  • Information without a credible source

Practical Activity

Access a web browser and perform the following searches. Review the search results and try to determine which information is trustworthy and which is not.

  • Weather today in Lusaka
  • Agricultural in Zambia
  • Educational resources near me
  • Local health services for Zambian residents

Introduction to Digital Communication

Learning Outcomes

Participants will be able to:

  • Use digital communication tools.
  • Communicate effectively online.
  • Demonstrate responsible digital behavior.
What is Digital Communication?

Digital communication is the exchange of information using digital technologies. Examples include:

  • Phone calls
  • SMS messages
  • Email
  • Messaging applications
  • Video calls
  • Social media
Common Digital Communication Tools
Tool Purpose
SMS Short text communication
Email Formal communication
WhatsApp Messaging, voice notes, group communication
Facebook Social networking and information sharing
Zoom/Google Meet Online meetings and training
Creating and Managing an Email Account

Participants learn how to:

  • Create an email account
  • Log in securely
  • Send and receive emails
  • Attach documents
  • Good Digital Communication Practices
  • Be respectful.
  • Use clear language.
  • Avoid sharing false information.
  • Protect personal information.
  • Think before posting online.
Digital Etiquette

Do:

  • ✓ Be polite
  • ✓ Respect others' opinions
  • ✓ Verify information before sharing
  • ✓ Respond professionally

Avoid:

  • ✖ Cyberbullying
  • ✖ Sharing offensive content
  • ✖ Spreading rumors
  • ✖ Sharing confidential information

Practical Activities: Communicating Digitally

Email Exercise
  • Create an email account.
  • Send an email to another participant.
  • Reply to received messages.
Messaging Exercise
  • Send text messages.
  • Share photos.
  • Send voice notes responsibly.
Role Play Activity

Scenario: You received a suspicious message claiming that you have won money. Think about the following:


  • Is the message genuine?
  • What warning signs can be identified?
  • What action should be taken?
