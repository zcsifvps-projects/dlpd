Digital Financial Literacy

Digital financial services have transformed the way people save, send, receive, and manage money. Mobile money, online banking, digital payments, and e-commerce platforms offer convenience and financial inclusion, particularly for individuals and communities with limited access to traditional banking services.

While digital financial tools provide many opportunities, users must also understand how to protect themselves from fraud, scams, and financial risks. This module equips participants with the knowledge and skills needed to use digital financial services safely and confidently.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, participants will be able to:

  • Understand the benefits and risks of digital financial services.
  • Practice safe mobile money and digital payment habits.
  • Identify common financial scams and fraud schemes.
  • Conduct secure online transactions.
  • Protect personal and financial information.

Mobile Money Safety

What is Mobile Money?

Mobile money is a financial service that allows users to store, send, and receive money using a mobile phone.

Benefits of Mobile Money

  • Convenient and accessible
  • Faster transactions
  • Reduced need to carry cash
  • Supports financial inclusion

Fraud Awareness

Common Types of Financial Fraud

Phishing

Fraudsters attempt to obtain personal or financial information through fake messages or websites.

Mobile Money Scams

Scammers may pretend to have sent money mistakenly and ask for refunds.

Investment Scams

Promises of unusually high returns with little or no risk.

Identity Theft

Criminals use stolen personal information to conduct fraudulent activities.

Warning Signs

  • Urgent requests for money
  • Unsolicited offers
  • Requests for PINs or passwords
  • Deals that seem too good to be true

Safe Online Transactions

StageWhat To Do
Before the TransactionVerify the seller or service provider; check website legitimacy; read reviews and ratings; confirm payment details carefully.
During the TransactionUse secure internet connections; avoid public Wi-Fi for financial transactions; verify transaction confirmations.
After the TransactionKeep receipts and records; review account statements; report unauthorized activities immediately.
