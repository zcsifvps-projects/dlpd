Online Safety and Cyber Hygiene

Module Objective: To enable participants to protect themselves, their personal information, and their digital devices from online threats by practicing safe and responsible digital behavior.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, participants will be able to:

  • Create and manage strong passwords.
  • Identify common online scams and phishing attempts.
  • Use social media platforms safely and responsibly.
  • Protect their personal information and privacy online.
  • Apply safe online practices when using digital devices and internet services.

Password Management

What is a Password?

A password is a secret combination of letters, numbers, and symbols used to protect access to digital accounts and devices. Passwords help prevent unauthorized individuals from accessing personal information, financial accounts, emails, and social media profiles.

Why Strong Passwords Matter

Weak passwords can be easily guessed or stolen, leading to:

  • Identity theft
  • Financial loss
  • Unauthorized access to accounts
  • Misuse of personal information

Characteristics of a Strong Password

A strong password should:

  • Be at least 12 characters long
  • Include uppercase and lowercase letters
  • Include numbers
  • Include symbols where possible
  • Be unique for each account

Examples

Weak Passwords Strong Passwords
✖ 123456 ✓ M@ngoTree!2026
✖ password ✓ MyVillage#Safe24
✖ Zambia123 ✓ Luangwa$River88
✖ qwerty ✓ Baobab@2026Grow

Password Best Practices

Do

  • ✓ Use different passwords for different accounts
  • ✓ Change passwords if you suspect compromise
  • ✓ Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) when available
  • ✓ Store passwords securely

Don't

  • ✖ Share passwords with others
  • ✖ Write passwords in public places
  • ✖ Use personal information such as birthdays or names
  • ✖ Reuse the same password everywhere

Create a strong password using this formula:

Favourite object + Symbol + Number + Memorable phrase

Scam and Phishing Awareness

What is an Online Scam?

An online scam is an attempt by criminals to trick people into giving away money, personal information, or access to accounts.

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of scam where attackers pretend to be trusted organizations, businesses, or individuals to steal information.

Common Scam Examples

Scam Type How It Works
Mobile Money Fraud A caller claims you have won a prize and asks for your PIN.
Fake Job Opportunities Messages promising jobs in exchange for payment.
Fake Lottery / Prize Notifications claiming you have won money you never entered to win.
Fake Customer Support Someone pretending to represent a bank, mobile network, or service provider.

Warning Signs of Scams

Be cautious when messages:

  • Create urgency
  • Request passwords or PINs
  • Promise large rewards
  • Ask for money upfront
  • Contain suspicious links

How to Stay Safe

  • Verify information independently
  • Never share PINs or passwords
  • Contact organizations through official channels
  • Ignore suspicious messages
  • Report scams to service providers

Social Media Safety

Understanding Social Media Risks

Social media platforms allow people to connect and share information, but they can also expose users to risks including identity theft, online harassment, scams, misinformation, and privacy breaches.

Safe Social Media Practices

Protect Personal Information — Avoid sharing:

  • National identification numbers
  • Banking information
  • Home addresses
  • Personal passwords

Review Privacy Settings — Regularly check who can:

  • View your profile
  • See your posts
  • Contact you

Think Before Posting — Ask yourself:

  • Is this information private?
  • Could this post harm me or someone else?
  • Am I comfortable with others seeing this?

Responding to Online Harassment

If someone behaves inappropriately:

  • Do not engage
  • Block the individual
  • Report the behavior
  • Seek support if necessary

Practical Exercise

Review privacy settings on your social media accounts and make appropriate adjustments. Check who can view your profile, see your posts, and contact you.

Privacy and Data Protection

What is Personal Information?

Personal information is any information that can identify an individual. Examples include full name, phone number, address, national ID number, bank account details, and photographs.

Why Privacy Matters

Protecting personal information helps prevent identity theft, fraud, financial loss, and unauthorized access.

Context Best Practice
On Devices Use screen locks; keep devices updated; install trusted applications only.
Online Share only necessary information; review privacy settings regularly; log out of shared devices.
On Public Wi-Fi Avoid accessing banking services; avoid sharing sensitive information; use trusted networks whenever possible.

Data Protection Principles

  • Know what information you share
  • Understand who can access your information
  • Give informed consent before sharing personal data

Safe Online Behavior

What is Cyber Hygiene?

Cyber hygiene refers to routine practices that help maintain digital security and reduce online risks. Just as personal hygiene protects health, cyber hygiene protects digital safety.

Good Cyber Hygiene Practices

Practice Why It Matters
Keep Devices Updated Software updates often fix security vulnerabilities.
Use Antivirus and Security Tools Install trusted security software where appropriate.
Download Carefully Only download applications from trusted sources.
Back Up Important Information Store copies of important files in safe locations.
Log Out After Use Especially when using shared or public devices.
Verify Before Sharing Avoid spreading misinformation or harmful content.

Responsible Online Behavior

  • Treat others respectfully online
  • Follow community guidelines
  • Avoid cyberbullying
  • Respect intellectual property and copyright
  • Use technology ethically
