About the Zambian Cyber Security Initiative Foundation

The Zambian Cyber Security Initiative Foundation (ZCSIF) is a Lusaka-based nonprofit dedicated to helping individuals, organizations, and communities stay safe, secure, and informed in the digital world. Our work spans public education, practical cybersecurity resources, and rights-based advocacy — all aimed at closing Zambia's digital divide and protecting people's online rights.


Beyond this training program, the Foundation shares practical cybersecurity guides, templates, and alerts for communities, newsrooms, and organizations across Zambia, and creates ongoing content to promote internet freedom and digital safety nationwide.

About Digital Bridges

As technology continues to transform how people communicate, learn, work, access services, and conduct business, digital skills have become essential for participation in modern society. However, many individuals and communities still face barriers to accessing and effectively using digital technologies. ZCSIF developed Digital Bridges to help bridge that gap by providing practical, accessible, and inclusive digital literacy education tailored to the needs of diverse communities across Zambia.


Digital Bridges aims to empower participants with the knowledge, skills, and confidence required to navigate the digital world safely, responsibly, and productively. Whether using a smartphone to communicate with family, accessing online information for education, conducting mobile money transactions, searching for employment opportunities, or promoting a small business online, digital literacy opens doors to new opportunities for personal and community development.


Special attention is given to promoting digital inclusion for women, youth, persons with disabilities, rural communities, and other underserved groups. By the end of the program, participants are better equipped to use digital tools confidently, protect themselves online, access information and services, and contribute positively to Zambia's growing digital economy.
