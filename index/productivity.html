Productivity and Work Readiness

Digital skills are increasingly required in education, employment, and business. Employers expect workers to communicate effectively, create documents, conduct research, and complete online applications. Developing these skills enhances employability and workplace productivity.

This module introduces practical digital tools that support learning, job searching, and career development.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, participants will be able to:

  • Create and edit basic digital documents.
  • Conduct effective online research.
  • Complete online application forms.
  • Develop professional CVs and digital profiles.

Basic Document Creation

What is a Digital Document?

A digital document is an electronic file used to create, store, and share information.

✉️ Letters 📄 Reports 🧑‍💼 CVs 📝 Meeting Notes

Basic Skills

  • Creating documents
  • Editing text
  • Formatting content
  • Saving and organizing files
  • Printing and sharing documents
🏷️
Use Clear Headings

Helps readers navigate your document easily.

🔍

Proofread before sharing or submitting.

💾
Save Work Regularly

Internet Research

What is Online Research?

Online research involves finding information using search engines and digital resources.

✓ Effective Search Techniques

  • Use specific keywords
  • Compare information from multiple sources
  • Evaluate source credibility
  • Check publication dates

📚 Reliable Sources

  • Educational institutions
  • Government websites
  • Professional organizations
  • Reputable news sources

Online Applications

Common Online Applications

  • Job applications
  • Scholarship applications
  • Business registrations
  • Service requests

✓ Application Tips

  • Read instructions carefully
  • Complete all required fields
  • Upload correct documents
  • Review information before submission

✖ Common Mistakes

  • Incorrect contact information
  • Missing documents
  • Typographical errors
  • Incomplete applications

CV and Digital Profile Development

What is a CV?

A Curriculum Vitae (CV) summarizes a person's education, skills, and experience.

Key Sections

Section Contains
Personal Details Name, contact information, location
Education Schools, qualifications, certificates
Work Experience Past roles, responsibilities, achievements
Skills Technical and personal skills relevant to the role
References Contacts who can vouch for your work

Creating a Professional Digital Profile

A digital profile may include a professional photo, skills and qualifications, career achievements, and contact information.

✖ Best Practices

  • Keep information updated.
  • Use professional language.
  • Highlight relevant skills and achievements.
