Introduction to Digital Literacy

Module Overview

Digital literacy is the ability to use digital technologies such as smartphones, computers, and the internet to access information, communicate, solve problems, and participate effectively in society. This module introduces learners to basic digital concepts and builds confidence in using digital tools safely and effectively.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, participants will be able to:

  • Define digital literacy and explain its importance.
  • Identify common digital devices and their uses.
  • Demonstrate basic smartphone and computer operations.
  • Navigate the internet to find information.
  • Use digital communication tools responsibly.

Understanding Digital Devices

Learning Outcomes

Participants will be able to:

  • Identify different digital devices.
  • Describe the functions of common devices.
  • Understand how digital devices support daily life.

What is a Digital Device?

A digital device is an electronic tool that helps people create, store, process, and share information.

Common Digital Devices

Device Purpose
Smartphone Communication, internet access, mobile banking, learning
Computer/Laptop Work, education, document creation, internet access
Tablet Learning, entertainment, communication
Feature Phone Calls, SMS, mobile money
Smart TV Accessing digital content and information

Smartphone and Computer Basics

Learning Outcomes

Participants will be able to:

  • Operate smartphones and computers safely.
  • Identify basic hardware components.
  • Perform simple device functions.
Parts of a Smartphone
  • Screen
  • Power button
  • Volume buttons
  • Camera
  • Charging port
  • SIM card slot
Basic Smartphone Functions
  • Turning the device on and off
  • Unlocking the screen
  • Making calls
  • Sending messages
  • Opening applications
  • Adjusting volume and brightness
Parts of a Computer
  • Monitor
  • Keyboard
  • Mouse
  • CPU/System Unit
  • Speakers
  • Webcam
Basic Computer Skills
  • Switching the computer on and off
  • Using a mouse
  • Opening and closing programs
  • Typing basic text
  • Saving files
Device Care and Maintenance
  • Keep devices clean and dry.
  • Avoid sharing passwords.
  • Charge devices safely.
  • Install updates when available.
  • Protect devices from theft and damage.

Practical Activities:

Smartphone Practice
  • Make a phone call using a smartphone.
  • Save a contact in the Contacts list.
  • Send a text message.
  • Take some photographs using a smartphone as your camera.
Computer Practice
  • Move the mouse around on the screen.
  • Open a document.
  • Type your name.
  • Save a document and then reopen the file.
Key Message

Digital skills improve with practice. Mistakes are part of learning.
